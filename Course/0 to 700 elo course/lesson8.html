Lesson-8: Castling


Castling is a special move in chess. It consists of moving the king two squares toward a rook on the same rank and then moving the rook to the square that the king passed over in a move. There are two types of castle: short castle and long castle.

If you want to castle then he have to maintain some rules of castling. You have to ensure the things-
  • Is the king in a check or not,
  • king has been moved once or not,
  • rook which he wants to use to castle has been moved once or not,
  • all squares in between king and rook is empty or not
Also you have to ensure that, the squares that king will use to go it must be free from check. If someone does short castle then their king and rook goes to kingside and if someone does long castle then their king and rook goes to queenside. Doing castling earlier is a golden rule of chess. By doing castling, someone can protect their king also activates their rook.
